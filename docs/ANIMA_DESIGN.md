# 1. Vision

## What is ANIMA?

ANIMA (Artificial Neural Intelligence for Multi-Agent Advancement) is an open-source Artificial Life Engine designed to simulate the emergence of intelligent life.

Instead of scripting the behavior of virtual beings, ANIMA creates a world governed by universal rules, allowing intelligence, society, and civilization to emerge naturally through interaction, learning, adaptation, and evolution.

Every individual begins with simple instincts and no predefined knowledge.

Through experience, memory, and interaction with the environment, individuals gradually learn how to survive, cooperate, reproduce, innovate, and eventually build civilizations.

The purpose of ANIMA is not to create a game.

The purpose is to build a believable digital world where intelligence is not programmed—it emerges.

# 2. Core Philosophy

The development of ANIMA follows a strict set of principles.

These principles define the identity of the project and should remain unchanged throughout its evolution.

## Reality Before Gameplay

The simulation always prioritizes realism over entertainment.

Every system should behave according to consistent natural laws.

## Emergence Over Scripts

Complex behavior should emerge naturally from simple interactions.

The engine should avoid hardcoded behaviors whenever general rules can produce the same result.

## Every Action Has Consequences

Every action changes the future.

Nothing happens without consequences.

## Knowledge Must Be Earned

Individuals are born without knowledge.

Everything must be learned through observation, experience, teaching, or experimentation.

## Survival Comes First

Every intelligent being first attempts to survive.

Higher goals only become possible after basic needs are satisfied.

## Death Is Part of Evolution

Death is a natural mechanism that enables adaptation and long-term evolution.

## The World Is Independent

The world continues to exist regardless of any individual.

No creature is the center of the simulation.

## Intelligence Emerges

Intelligence is never directly programmed.

It emerges from perception, memory, reasoning, learning, and interaction.

## Engine Before Interface

The simulation engine is completely independent from the graphical interface.

The UI observes the world but never controls it.

## Simplicity Creates Complexity

Simple universal rules should produce complex societies.

Whenever possible, simplicity is preferred over special-case logic.

# 3. World Rules

The world is the foundation of ANIMA.

Everything that exists inside the simulation follows the same universal laws.

The world existed before any individual was born and will continue after every individual dies.

## Universal World Rules

1. The world exists independently of every creature.

2. Time never stops.

3. Every object occupies a physical location.

4. Distance always has a cost.

5. Every movement consumes time and energy.

6. Resources are finite.

7. Natural resources regenerate according to environmental conditions.

8. Every action changes the world.

9. Every event leaves traces.

10. Information is local.

11. No individual possesses global knowledge.

12. Weather affects survival.

13. Seasons affect food production.

14. Rivers, forests and mountains influence movement.

15. Every living creature follows the same physical laws.

16. Birth requires parents.

17. Every living organism ages.

18. Every organism eventually dies.

19. Evolution happens over generations.

20. Nothing appears without a cause.
